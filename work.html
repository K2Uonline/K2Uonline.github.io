My Work Experience

Leidos

I am incredibly grateful for my time at Leidos. I worked alongside highly experienced electrical engineers who set high expectations for me while answering all my questions. This experience taught me the importance of designing modular, future-proof systems.

X-ray Control System

Designed a control system for vehicle X-ray portals to connect the X-ray source, detectors, interlocks, and user PC:

  • Used a modular design with an STM32 microcontroller on the main board.
  • Integrated power and I/O boards with 2x 80-pin Samtec headers for adaptability to different X-ray sources (LINAC and Betatrons).
  • Ensured future-proofing for systems requiring modular changes.

Electrical-Optical Transceiver

Developed a communication system for clocking packets between X-ray sources and detectors:

  • Converted signals between single-ended TTL, RS-232 differential, and optical logic.
  • Improved reliability and versatility of communication systems.

L3Harris

At L3Harris, I worked on cutting-edge optics-based microelectronics. The projects allowed me to design low-power, high-reliability systems for precision applications.

Micro-actuator System

Designed a modular micro-actuator system with power and communication over fiber:

  • Implemented Power-over-Fiber (400mW) using SMF-28 base station and optical TX/RX modules.
  • Integrated photovoltaics, photodiodes, and supercapacitors for efficient power storage.
  • Controlled a 2mm squiggle motor using an I2C bus over Mill-Max pins.
  • Utilized NXP KL03 microcontroller for low-power operation and sleep modes.

Microscope Illuminator

Designed a flexible patterned illuminator with precision control and optimized drivers:

  • Ensured high flexibility for dynamic lighting patterns.
  • Designed efficient drivers for seamless operation in optical systems.
